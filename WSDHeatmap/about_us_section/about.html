About Our Data

Analyzing demographic trends using IPUMS ACS and CPS data to uncover socioeconomic patterns.

Project Mission

This project utilizes IPUMS (Integrated Public Use Microdata Series) data from the American Community Survey (ACS) and Current Population Survey (CPS) to analyze demographic, economic, and social trends. We combine these authoritative census datasets with modern data visualization techniques to provide actionable insights into population characteristics and labor market dynamics.

Data Sources

American Community Survey (ACS)

Annual Census Bureau survey with 1-5 year estimates covering income, education, housing, and demographic characteristics of the U.S. population. Provides detailed cross-tabulations at multiple geographic levels.

Current Population Survey (CPS)

Monthly labor force survey conducted jointly by Census Bureau and Bureau of Labor Statistics. Tracks employment, unemployment, and occupational data with detailed demographic breakdowns.

IPUMS Integration

Harmonized microdata with consistent variable definitions across years, enabling time-series analysis and comparative demographic research across multiple decades.

Methodology

  • Data Extraction: Filtered IPUMS samples using Census Bureau weighting and stratification variables
  • Variable Harmonization: Standardized coding schemes across ACS and CPS for consistency
  • Statistical Analysis: Applied survey-appropriate weighting and confidence intervals
  • Visualization: Created interactive charts and geographic dashboards
  • Interpretation: Contextualized findings with demographic and economic trends

Key Datasets

ACS 1-Year
Annual estimates with larger samples
ACS 5-Year
Pooled estimates for smaller areas
CPS ASEC
Annual Social and Economic Supplement
CPS ORG
Outgoing Rotation Groups monthly data

Key Variables Analyzed

  • Demographics: Age, gender, race/ethnicity, citizenship status
  • Economic: Income, employment status, occupation, industry
  • Education: Educational attainment, enrollment status
  • Housing: Housing tenure, costs, unit characteristics (ACS)
  • Labor Force: Hours worked, wages, union membership (CPS)

Tools & Technologies

  • Data Processing: Python, Pandas, NumPy for survey data analysis
  • Statistical Analysis: R, SAS survey procedures for weighted estimates
  • Visualization: D3.js, Plotly for interactive dashboards
  • Web Stack: HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript for responsive design
  • Data Source: IPUMS (www.ipums.org)
